Mac DeMarco

The man who created "jizz jazz"

Mac DeMarco performing at Gothic Theatre in 2014

Here's a time line of Mac Demarco's life:

  • 1990 - born in Duncan, British Columbia, Canada, and raised in Edmonton, Alberta.
  • 2008 - graduates from high school in Edmonton in 2008, and moves to Vancouver.
  • 2009 - releases a self-produced album, Heat Wave, calling himself Makeout Videotape, signs to Unfamiliar Records, and tours with Vancouver band Japandroids.
  • 2011 - moves from Vancouver to Montreal to begin recording as a solo artist. Failing to find work as a musician, he participates in medical experiments for money and works on a road paving crew.
  • 2012 - he released an EP titled Rock and Roll Night Club.
  • 2014 - announces the release of his upcoming second album, Salad Days, along with debuting the lead single "Passing Out Pieces" and receives the "Best New Music" designation from Pitchfork.
  • 2015 - releases a mini album titled Another One and releases a 9 track instrumental album titled Some Other Ones and calls it a "BBQ soundtrack".
